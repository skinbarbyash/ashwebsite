How Often Should You Get a Facial?

The recommended frequency of facials, typically every 4-6 weeks, aligns with the natural skin cell turnover cycle, ensuring consistent rejuvenation and maintenance of skin health. However, individual skin types and needs may warrant adjustments to this timeframe. Oily or acne-prone skin may benefit from more frequent facials to manage excess oil and breakouts, while sensitive skin may require longer intervals between treatments to prevent irritation.

Day 1: Immediate improvement! Skin is glowing, hydrated, rejuvenated and nourished

Day 2-3: Your skin will be looking plumper thanks to the booster circulation from your facial

Day 21-28: Your cells have begun to regenerate, and you can see full benefits from your facial

Week 4-6: The natural skin cell turnover cycle is around 28 days, so getting a facial every 4-6 weeks will help to keep your skin looking fresh and rejuvenated

Dermaplaning Facial

Dermaplaning is a physical exfoliation method that requires the use of a medical grade scalpel to gently exfoliate the top layer of the skin which consists of dead skin cell, oil, hair and debris. This treatment is designed to improve the overall appearance of the skin by making it look brighter, smoother, more youthful, and radiant. During the dermaplaning process, we will be removing dead cells, scar tissue, and other debris that may be causing the skin's surface to appear uneven. This process also temporarily removes fine vellus hair, leaving the skin feeling extremely smooth. Also, exposes newer, healthier skin cells by clearing away damaged skin cells caused by environment toxins, irritants, and sun damage. Dermaplaning can be beneficial for a variety of skin types and concerns, including hyperpigmentation, dull or uneven skin tone, fine lines and wrinkles and acne. It can also help to improve the effectiveness and penetration of skincare products by removing the barrier of dead skin cells on the surface of the skin.
